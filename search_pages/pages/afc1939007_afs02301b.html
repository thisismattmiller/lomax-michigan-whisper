  • Alright, well, do I remember when first I went to sea?
  • I was a gay light-hearted youth as happy as could be.
  • For I longed to lead a sailor's life among the true and brave.
  • My heart is filled with my content as boundless as waves.
  • I have seen strange sights and seen since then, but nothing to my mind.
  • Like when I saw a tall ship per sailor spread to the wind.
  • I have roamed for many the night of day, midst gloomy mists and seas. But never do I once regret when first I went to sea.
  • I have saw the waves that round me dash, what a joy and sin are made.
  • I have all time saw a ship at sea, her bow were carried away. But never do I wish to change those things, where'er I be.
  • Or never do I once regret when first I went to sea.
  • I have saw the waves that round me dash, where men could never sail. I have saw the winds and heard them rinse and tatter every sail.
  • For I faced my death one hundred times, but providence proved good.
  • And years and years the storm has braved, and tempest I have stoned.
  • Oh, I have kept a midnight watch beneath those stormy skies.
  • I have listened to those seabirds scream their loud, abysmal cries. But on this dark domestic sea there is a secret still,
  • that only sailors know and fear. There's none but them can tell.
  • [The End]
