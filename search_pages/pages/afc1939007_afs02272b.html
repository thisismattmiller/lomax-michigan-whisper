  • >> Oh, of all the tradesmen going, sure, a peddler's my delight.
  • For if he rambles all the day, he'll comfort you at night.
  • With his little pack up on his back, he'll travel to and fro.
  • And he's called the Jolly Rover, wherever he will go.
  • He's a wavering London dairy, oh, a shoemaker in Sorbann.
  • Hair merchant in Lundivaddy, and a brewer in Kilrayne.
  • For he does brew good humming ale and loves a pretty maid.
  • And when he gets into Belfast, he's a butcher by his trade.
  • Oh, as he does roam the nations, it's fancy to pursue.
  • Gengene's occupation for every face of snooze.
  • Oh, kissing pretty fair ones, wherever he will roam.
  • And still at night, his little love is true, when he'll return home.
