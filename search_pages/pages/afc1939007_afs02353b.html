  • Come all you true boys from the river,
  • Come and listen to me for a while,
  • And I will relate to the story,
  • Of my true friend and chum Johnny Stiles.
  • We were camped on the wild mustard river,
  • Down by the old reservoir dam.
  • When two of our one mourners,
  • We rode from our blanket,
  • On the rocks there we spied a big jam.
  • As soon as we'd eaten our breakfast,
  • We pulled for the head of the dam,
  • While two of our boys took the pole trail,
  • For to flood from the reservoir dam.
  • The water came rocking and howling,
  • With PVs and pike poles we pried.
  • Till at last we gained faith in the middle,
  • And like rightlings from there he did fly.
  • While riding her into dead water,
  • His foot it got caught in a jam.
  • He never once squealed till an under,
  • For he always had plenty of sand.
  • Five hours we worked at hard labor,
  • Till the sweat from our brow it did pour.
  • We dragged his dead body from an under,
  • But it didn't look like Johnny anymore.
  • He would crunch from his heels to his middle,
  • And his head was cut off in the jam.
  • We buried him down by the river,
  • For the larks and the whipper will sing.
