  • AFS Recording Number 2323, Side A.
  • Come all you young, shanty boys, come listen to what I say.
  • I want you to tell the story that happened on that day.
  • It was of six young shanty boys, so gallant, sea, and brave.
  • So to break the jam on Gary's rock, they met with the watery grave.
  • But it happened on a Sunday, it was in the month of May,
  • when our foreman came on to his camp, and this to us did say,
  • Turn out, my boys, turn out with your hearts so full of cheer,
  • and we'll break the jam on Gary's rock, and then for town we'll tear.
  • Oh, some of them were willing, while others they hung back.
  • To work upon a Sunday, they did not think was right,
  • but six of our bold Canadian boys did volunteer and go,
  • to break the jam on Gary's rock with their foreman, young Monroe.
  • Oh, they had not rolled off many a log, when the boss to them did say,
  • I tell you on your guard, my boys, for this jam will soon give way.
  • He hardly had those few words spoken, when the jam did break and go,
  • and carried away those six brave youths with their foreman, young Monroe.
  • Oh, the news went to the shanty, and every man turned out,
  • to search for those dead bodies to the river they drew near,
  • amongst those drifting bodies to search that grief and woe,
  • all cut and mangled on the beach by the head of young Monroe.
  • Oh, they picked up from the watery stage, and threw the grazing hares.
  • There was a form amongst them, her cries did rend the airs.
  • There was a form amongst them, a maid from Saginaw town.
  • Her mourns and cries did rend the skies, for her lover that was drowned.
  • Miss Clara was a widow, she lived by the River Bend.
  • She had a Dr. Mary, she was the raftman's friend.
  • The rest of his wages, the boss did pay to her.
  • The rest of his wages was paid.
